Tourister in Saudi Arabia

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia has witnessed a fantastic improvement in the last few years. There is growth in the tourism sector in Saudi Arabia. Tourism in Saudi Arabia has grown to be a worthy resource for the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. Although most tourists still visit Saudi Arabia for religious pilgrimages, Saudi Arabia is the second biggest tourist destination in the Middle East. Many tourists visit Saudi Arabia to see the historical places and its Museums. In addition to archeological places in Saudi Arabia, there are many modern places you can visit and enjoy. Interest in tourism comes with Saudi Vision 2030 that Saudi Arabia will be an international tourist destination with over 20 million tourists. In this article, we will give you all that you want to know about Saudi Arabia. We will focus on the tourism sector.

Tourism in Saudi Arabia and the Vision 2030

Tourism in Saudi Arabia is one of the goals of Vision 2030, in which it is expected to receive 100 million visits to the Kingdom annually. There are many government efforts to develop the tourism sector in Saudi Arabia to become compatible with Vision 2030 and achieve its goals, the most important of which is increasing the domestic product to more than 10% and making the Kingdom one of the 5 most welcoming countries for tourists. With the vision of the Kingdom 2030, Saudi Arabia has provided many job opportunities to employ Saudi youth and contribute to the development of the Kingdom, especially in the tourism sector, through the establishment of many tourism and entertainment projects in the Kingdom to attract the largest number of tourists.

Geographical location

Extending across most of the northern and central Arabian Peninsula, Saudi Arabia is a young country that is heir to a rich history. In its western highlands, along the Red Sea, lies the Hejaz, which is the cradle of Islam and the site of that religion’s holiest cities, Mecca and Medina. In the country’s geographic heartland is a region known as Najd (“Highland”), a vast arid zone that until recent times was populated by nomadic tribes. To the east, along the Persian Gulf, are the country’s abundant oil fields that, since the 1960s, have made Saudi Arabia synonymous with petroleum wealth. Those three elements—religion, tribalism, and untold wealth—have fueled the country’s subsequent history
